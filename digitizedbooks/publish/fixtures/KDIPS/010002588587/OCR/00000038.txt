LIFE AND PROPHECIES OF ZECHARIAH. 33
IV.
The Third Division of Prophecies.
The third and last division of Zechariah's
prophecies begins with chapter xi of his
book.
In the preceding prophecies the people
are encouraged by the prospect of a restored
and possibly reunited nation. Their country
would once more be theirs; their beloved
Jerusalem would be rehabilitated, and that
grandest of all considerations, the Temple,
would be rebuilt. It was true that "many
of the priests and Levites and chief of the
fathers, who were ancient men, that had
seen the -first house, when the foundation
of this house was laid before their eyes, wept
with a loud voice" (Ezraiii:i2) ; but "The
glory of this latter house shall be greater
than of the former, saith the Lord of hosts;
and in this place will I give peace, saith the
Lord of hosts" (Haggai ii:9).
